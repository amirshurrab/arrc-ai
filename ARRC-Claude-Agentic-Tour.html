How to demo the agentic side

A 15-minute session that reframes what they think AI is

For a room that already prompts, the goal isn't to impress with writing — it's to shift the mental model from "tool I operate" to "teammate I delegate to."

1

Name a job they own

Pick one recurring, multi-step workflow in the room — the bid response, the SteerCo pack. Something they personally lose hours to.

2

Delegate, don't prompt

Give Claude the goal + access to one real system. Then stop typing. Let them watch it plan and take the next step itself.

3

Show the approval gate

Let it reach a "send" or "post" step and pause for sign-off. This is the trust moment for government-grade work.

4

Hand over the artifacts

End on the real .docx / .pptx / .xlsx it produced and saved. Tangible output closes the gap between demo and adoption.

My recommendation, in one line: demo delegation, not dictation. Run one blueprint that mirrors a workflow the room owns — the Bid Agent and PMO SteerCo Agent are the most visceral — connected to a real system, live. The discovery moment is watching Claude choose its next step without being re-prompted. Then show the capability stack so each person can imagine their own agent. Close on governance: humans stay the approvers on anything irreversible — that's what makes agents safe to adopt for national-scale clients.
