How to present this to the room

A 15-minute demo that lands every time

The recommended way to show Claude to an audience that's new to AI — make it about them, make it live, make it concrete.

1

Open on their pain

Start with one task everyone in the room dreads — "the 70-page RFP that lands at 4pm." Get a nod before you show anything.

2

Run it live, on real work

Paste an actual (non-confidential) ARRC-style document and run a demo above. Live beats slides — let them watch it happen.

3

Hand them the keyboard

Give one skeptic a copy-paste prompt from the "Try it" section and let them run their own task. Belief comes from doing.

4

Close on judgment

End clear: Claude drafts and structures; the consultant decides, verifies, and owns quality. It scales people — it doesn't replace them.

My recommendation, in one line: don't pitch "AI" — pitch ARRC's own deliverables, produced in minutes. Lead with the RFP triage and the Arabic⇄English summary (instantly relatable, visibly impressive), then let the room drive. Keep a curated, non-confidential sample document ready so every demo runs on something they recognize. The wow isn't that Claude writes — it's that it writes their work.
